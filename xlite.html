FrSky X-Lite

The FrSky X-Lite is the best TX for thumbers and my new favorite controller.

The FrSky X-Lite is the ultimate radio controller for drone racing. Its comfortable form factor is incredible and provides a new level of comfort that even the Turnigy Evolution and FlySky Nirvana could not acheive. After using the FlySky Fs-i6, the Turnigy Evolution and the FrSky QX7, I thought that I had tried everything and returned to my modded Fs-i6 until I saw this and I was excited. There had been rumors of this controller coming out ages and it was finally out. A few reviewers had made videos about it and it seemed great so I ordered it. Inside the box it came with a nice carry case and two gimbal protectors. I flashed the newest firmware and a new sound pack and plugged it in to Liftoff. It felt amazing and actually improved my racing skill due to the smaller gimbals and shorter sticks. For a pincher smaller gimbals and sticks are a bad thing but for many thumbers, the gimbals allow for the use of the whole gimbal rather than it being to large. After a short while of getting used to it, I could pull off many tricks I couldn't do and improved my racing significantly.

The X-Lite is alot smaller than the traditional TX and is more portable than other large controllers such as the QX7. However this means that FrSky could not fit a full size JR module and used their new JR mini module. This meant that only FrSkys new R9M Lite could work with the X-Lite but soon enough there was a way to convert the smaller module into its full size counterpart through a simple adaptor. This means that the TBS Crossfire and other JR modules and now work with the X-Lite. Another issue that is yet to be fixed is the lack of a internal battery or battery charging circuit which means that the uncommon 18500 style batteries have to be bought seperatly and taken out of the controller to be charged. These issues have been fixed but their solutions are arguably worse than the problem itself. The new X-Lite Pro has an internal battery charging circuit but the newer FrSky radios are using ACCESS protocol which is much worse than the previous ACCST protocol and the new FrSky radios should not be bought (X9-Lite, X-Lite S, X-Lite Pro). The much more popular 18650 batteries can be used with the X-Lite if you use custom battery caps but they are cheaply made and ugly.
