Lenovo Windows Mixed Reality

An alternative to the HTC Vive and the Oculus Rift, the Windows Mixed Reality provides a easier and cheaper experience to VR with little disadvantages.

Currently there are 4 large groups of PCVR, the HTC Vive, Oculus Rift, Windows Mixed Reality (MWR) and the Valve Index. Of these, only the WMR uses the headset to track the controllers rather than sensors positioned around the room. This means that in a situation where you cant use sensors such as a small room or one crowded with objects, the WMR is your only viable method of using VR. Another advantage of WMR is is low cost as You can find WMRs being sold for very cheap prices during sales and you will still be getting a full VR experience.

The naming of the WMR isnt very acurate as it isnt Mixed reality and is just Virtual reality but using cameras to track the controllers rather than sensors. However this can be considered a negative as the tracking of the WMR isnt as good as the HTC Vive or Oculus Rift but it is more than enough to experience VR and has been improved greatly over the years. The tracking infront of the headset is perfect unless you bring the controllers to your face as that is where the cameras are positioned. I never found this to be a problem though as you never lift your hands that close to your face even when aiming down sights in a shooter. The tracking does suffer behind you back where the cameras cannot see but if you dont leave you controllers there for long the gyro and accelerometer will estimate where you controllers are located. Due to the constant software updates from Microsoft the WMR platform has improved drastically from where it started and is now just as good as any other VR.

The Lenovo VR is one of the 8 current WMR headsets and is currently the best value VR headset for its price. The Lenovo, Acer, Dell, Asus and HPs first WMR headsets all have the same specs however there are some important differences in contruction and their actual performance. The Acer headset is the worst headset of the WMR lineup with it being uncomfortable and a bad viewing experience. The Lenovo is very similar to the Dell headset with the main difference that the Lenovo is 100g lighter. The HP is even lighter but it has a smaller "sweet spot" or the area in the lens where everything is in focus. The Asus is also very similar to the Lenovo but has a higher build quality at a higher price. The Samsung Odyssy and Odyssy Plus are higher quality WMR headsets which use OLED screens instead of LCD and have built in headphones. The main difference between normal and the plus is the anti Screen Door Effect (anti-SDE) on the plus reducing eye strain when reading text in VR. The newest WMR is the HP Reverb which features a ultra high resolution screen eliminating the screen door effect and making everything sharper.
